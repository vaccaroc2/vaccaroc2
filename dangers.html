Dangers of Public Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fi networks are often open and unsecured, making users vulnerable to:

  • Man-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks: Hackers intercept communication between users and websites.
  • Packet sniffing: Attackers capture unencrypted data traveling through the network.
  • Rogue access points: Fake Wi-Fi networks trick users into connecting.
  • Malware injection: Hackers deliver malicious code through insecure connections.
